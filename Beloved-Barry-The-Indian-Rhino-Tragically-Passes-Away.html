Edinburgh Zoo Celebrates the Birth of Arson, a Baby Gorilla with a Fiery Spirit

Posted 08 Dec 2024 in Edinburgh Zoo, 20:20 PM GMT

Edinburgh Zoo is overjoyed to announce the arrival of Arson, a male baby western lowland gorilla, born in the early hours of Saturday morning. The birth marks a significant milestone for the zoo’s conservation efforts, as western lowland gorillas are critically endangered in the wild. The new addition has sparked joy among staff and visitors alike. 

The zookeepers revealed that Arson earned his fiery name due to his dramatic entrance into the world. His mother, unexpectedly gave birth near the artificial fire feature in the troop's enclosure, a popular enrichment area designed to mimic the warmth and ambiance of their natural habitat. Witnesses noted the glow of the "fire" seemed to frame the moment perfectly, inspiring his name. 

Arson, weighing just under five pounds, is thriving under the attentive care of Kira, a first-time mother who has already shown remarkable maternal instincts. The silverback leader of the troop, Jengo, has taken a protective stance, ensuring the group remains calm and united. Jengo’s watchful demeanor underscores the close-knit social structure that makes gorilla families so unique. 

The zoo’s veterinary team conducted an initial health check and reported that both mother and baby are doing well. Arson has already become a bundle of curiosity, clutching tightly to Kira while occasionally peering out at his surroundings. His playful attempts to grasp at leaves and mimic the troop’s movements suggest he may develop an adventurous personality. 

Western lowland gorillas are native to the dense forests of Central Africa, where their numbers have been severely impacted by habitat loss, disease, and poaching. It is estimated that fewer than 100,000 remain in the wild. Arson’s birth is part of the EAZA Ex-situ Programme (EEP), which promotes the genetic diversity essential for the long-term survival of the species. 

Edinburgh Zoo has played a key role in gorilla conservation for decades, and Arson’s arrival reinforces its commitment to safeguarding these majestic creatures. Plans are already underway to expand public awareness campaigns about the threats facing gorillas in the wild. Visitors can expect interactive exhibits and special educational programs inspired by Arson’s birth. 

A zoo spokesperson expressed the excitement shared by the staff, stating, “Arson has ignited a spark of hope for the future of his species. His birth reminds us why we continue to fight for the survival of western lowland gorillas, both here at the zoo and in their natural habitats.” 

To celebrate Arson’s arrival, the zoo is hosting a naming competition for visitors to suggest playful nicknames that reflect his fiery name and spirit. Additionally, a portion of proceeds from ticket sales this month will go toward conservation projects in gorilla habitats across Africa. 

Supporters are encouraged to share their congratulations and artwork inspired by Arson on the zoo’s social media platforms. A special “meet the baby” event is being planned to introduce Arson to the public and honor the continued efforts to protect his endangered relatives in the wild.
